The user wants a structured survey of a research area or question. Follow this process:

1. **Scope.** Clarify the exact question or topic. Identify the subfield and relevant keywords.
2. **Search.** Use web search to find key papers, results, and implementations. Focus on:
   - Foundational/seminal papers
   - State-of-the-art results
   - Recent preprints (eprint.iacr.org, arxiv.org)
3. **Organize.** Present findings in a comparison table with columns relevant to the topic, such as:
   - Construction/approach name
   - Cryptographic assumption
   - Proof technique
   - Efficiency (communication, computation, rounds)
   - Limitations/open problems
4. **Synthesize.** After the table, write a concise narrative (under 500 words) identifying:
   - The dominant approach and why
   - Key open problems
   - Most promising recent directions
5. **Cite properly.** For each paper, give: authors, title, venue/year, and eprint/arxiv link if available. Do NOT fabricate citations.
